Jon Atkinson

Head Coach

Jon Atkinson is the Head Coach of Charlottesville Alliance FC for the inaugural 2018 Season. Atkinson brings expertise from his long coaching career at Longwood University where he has been the head coach for twelve seasons and was chosen as the Big South Coach of the Year in 2013. Prior to his appointment to head at Longwood in 2005, Atkinson spent two seasons as an assistant for the Lancers. Atkinson holds a US Soccer Coaching A License and serves on the Board of Directors for the Thunder FC soccer program in Farmville, Virginia.

Atkinson is a native of Newcastle, England and played professionally at Sunderland and Darlington FC. Atkinson will be accompanied at Alliance FC by his Longwood assistant coach, Sam Avery, who has served as Atkinson’s assistant for the past 3 years.

He has two Bachelor’s degrees from English universities and an MBA from West Virginia Wesleyan. He is married to Nikki, and together they have one daughter and three sons.

David Deaton
Executive Director

David Deaton has worked for nearly 20 years in both for-profit and non-profit companies, where his tenure has focused on pricing, forecasting, market analysis, market research, and strategic planning. David has led significant soccer community outreach including collaborations with Boys and Girls Club and International Rescue Committee, mentoring for refugees interested in soccer, soccer infrastructure development, free weekly soccer clinics in low-income communities, and most notably managed Aromas Cafe FC during a highly successful 2015 Open Cup campaign.

Through Sticks' community partnerships, Chris has had the opportunity to support and work with a variety of non-profits, such as City Schoolyard Gardens and Girls on the Run. Connor Crook

Jason Fargo
Director of Coaching

Jason Fargo has worked for the past 12 years in youth soccer as well as academia as a program director, coach, and educator. While working as a youth soccer program director Jason was responsible for managing schedules, players, and coaches. Implementing soccer ideas, tactics, and philosophies into the program as well as building relationships with other soccer professionals in the area and state. Jason currently holds his USSF "B" and "National Youth" licenses and has coached high level youth players for local youth soccer clubs and Virginia Youth Soccer Associations (VYSA) Olympic Development Program (ODP).

Price Thomas
